LAMENTATIONS OF JEREMIAH

CHAPTER 1

And it came to pass after that Israel was taken captive and Jerusalem made desolate, that Jeremiah sat weeping and lamented this lamentation over Jerusalem and said,

1‘How doth the city sit solitary, she that was full of people! She is become as a widow that was magnified among the nations, and ruler among the provinces; she is become tributary. 2She wept sore in the night, and her tears were on her cheeks; among all her lovers there is none to comfort her. All they that were her friends have dealt treacherously with her; they are become her enemies. 3Judah is gone into captivity because of her affliction, and because of the greatness of her servitude; she sat among the nations; she hath found no rest; all her pursuers have overtaken her among her oppressors. 4The ways of Zion do mourn, because there are none that come to the feast; all her gates are ruined; her priests groan; her virgins are led captive, and she is in bitterness in herself. 5They that oppress her are become the chief, and her enemies have prospered; for the LORD hath afflicted her because of the multitude of her ungodliness; her children are gone into captivity before the face of the oppressor. 6And from the daughter of Zion all her beauty is departed; her princes are become like rams that find no pasture, and they are gone without strength before the face of the pursuer. 7Jerusalem remembered the days of her humiliation and of her rejection, all her desirable things that were from the days of old, when her people fell into the hands of the oppressor, and there was none to help her. When her enemies saw, they did laugh at her habitation. 8Jerusalem hath grievously sinned; therefore she is come into tribulation. All that honoured her have humbled her, for they have seen her shame; yea, while she herself groaneth, she also turned away her face. 9Her filthiness was from her feet, she remembered not her last end; she hath brought down her boastings; there is none to comfort her. O LORD, behold mine affliction, for the enemy hath magnified himself. 10The oppressor hath stretched out his hand upon all the things of her desire, for she hath seen the heathen enter into her sanctuary, whom Thou didst command that they should not enter into Thy congregation. 11All her people groan as they seek bread; they have given the things of her desire for meat to restore their souls. See, O LORD, and consider that she is become dishonoured. 12All ye that pass by the way, turn and see if there be any sorrow like unto my sorrow which hath befallen me. The LORD that did speak by me hath afflicted me in the day of His fierce anger. 13From His height above hath He sent fire into my bones; He hath brought it down. He hath spread a net for my feet, He hath turned me back; He hath made me desolate and mourning all the day. 14He hath watched over my transgressions; they are wreathed about my hands; they are come up upon my neck. My strength hath failed, for the LORD hath laid pains upon my hands; I shall not be able to stand. 15The LORD hath removed all my mighty men from the midst of me; He hath called a season against me to crush my choice men; the LORD hath trodden a winepress for the virgin daughter of Judah; for these things I weep. 16Mine eye hath poured out water, because He that should comfort me, that should restore my soul, hath been removed far from me; my sons are put away, because the enemy prevailed. 17Zion hath spread forth her hands, and there is none to comfort her. The LORD hath commanded concerning Jacob; his oppressors are round about him; Jerusalem is become as a menstruous woman among them. 18The LORD is righteous, for I have provoked His mouth; hear, all ye people, and behold my sorrow; my virgins and my young men are gone into captivity. 19I called to my lovers, but they deceived me; my priests and my elders failed in the city, for they sought them meat to restore their souls, and found 20 it not. Behold, O LORD, for I am in distress; my bowels are troubled; mine heart is turned within me, for I have grievously rebelled. Abroad the sword hath bereaved me, even as death at home. 21Hear then, for I groan; there is none to comfort me; all mine enemies have heard of my troubles and are glad for that Thou hast done it. Thou hast brought on the day; Thou hast called the time; they are become like unto me. 22Let all their wickedness come before Thee, and glean Thou them, even as they have made a gleaning for all my transgressions; for my groanings are many, and my heart is afflicted with grief.

CHAPTER 2

‘How hath the LORD covered the daughter of Zion with darkness in His anger! He hath cast down from heaven unto the earth the glory of Israel, and remembered not His footstool in the day of His anger. 2The LORD hath overwhelmed her as in the sea, and hath not spared. He hath thrown down in His wrath all the beauties of Jacob; He hath brought down to the ground the strongholds of the daughter of Judah; He hath profaned her king and her princes. 3He hath broken off in His fierce anger all the horn of Israel; He hath turned back His right hand from before the enemy, and hath kindled in Jacob a flame like fire, which hath devoured all things round about. 4He hath bent His bow like an opposing enemy; He hath strengthened His right hand as an adversary, and destroyed all the desirable things of mine eyes in the tabernacle of the daughter of Zion; He poured out His fury like fire. 5The LORD is become as an enemy; He hath drowned Israel; He hath drowned her palaces; He hath laid in ruins her strongholds, and hath multiplied in the daughter of Judah humiliation upon humiliation. 6And He hath laid open His tabernacle, as it were of a vine; He hath marred His feast. The LORD hath caused to be forgotten the feast and the Sabbath, which He had appointed in Zion, and hath vexed in the indignation of His anger the king and the priest and the prince. 7The LORD hath cast off His altar; He hath shaken off His sanctuary; by the hand of the enemy hath He crushed the wall of her palaces. They have made a noise in the house of the LORD, as in the day of a solemn feast. 8And He hath turned back to destroy the wall of the daughter of Zion; He hath stretched out a line; He hath not withdrawn His hand from destroying. Therefore the rampart mourned, and the inner wall languished with it. 9Her gates were sunk into the ground; He hath destroyed and broken her bars; her king and her princes are among the Gentiles. There is law no more; her prophets also saw no vision from the LORD. 10The elders of the daughter of Zion sat upon the ground; they kept silence; they have cast up dust upon their heads; they have girded themselves with sackcloth; the virgin princesses of Jerusalem have they brought down to the ground. 11Mine eyes did fail with tears; my bowels were troubled; my glory was poured upon the earth for the destruction of the daughter of my people, while the infant and the suckling swooned in the streets of the city. 12They said to their mothers, “Where is bread and wine?” when they swooned as the wounded in the streets of the city, when their soul was poured out into their mother’s bosom. 13What thing shall I take to witness for thee, or what thing shall I liken to thee, O daughter of Jerusalem? Who shall save and comfort thee, O virgin daughter of Zion? For the cup of thy ruin is enlarged; who can heal thee? 14Thy prophets have seen vanities, and foolish things for thee, and they have not uncovered thine iniquity to turn back thy captivity, but have seen for thee vain burdens, and causes of banishment. 15All that pass by the way clapped their hands at thee; they hissed and wagged their head at the daughter of Jerusalem. Is this is the city of which they say, “The crown of joy of all the earth?” 16All thine enemies have opened their mouth against thee; they have hissed and gnashed their teeth, and have said, “We have swallowed her up. Certainly this is the day that we looked for; we have found, we have seen it.” 17The LORD hath done that which He had purposed; He hath accomplished His word that He had commanded from the days of old. He hath thrown down, and hath not spared, and He hath caused the enemy to rejoice over thee; He hath set up the horn of him that oppressed thee. 18Their heart cried unto the LORD, “O walls of Zion, pour down tears like torrents day and night; give thyself no rest; let not the daughter of thine eye cease.” 19Arise, rejoice in the night, in the beginning of the watch; pour out thine heart like water before the face of the LORD; lift up thy hands toward Him, for the life of thy young children that faint for hunger at the top of all the streets. 20Behold, O LORD, and consider for whom Thou hast gathered thus. Shall the women eat the fruit of their womb? The cook hath made a gathering; shall the infants sucking at the breast be slain? Wilt Thou slay the priest and the prophet in the sanctuary of the LORD? 21The child and the old man lay on the ground in the street; my virgins and my young men are gone into captivity. Thou hast slain them with the sword and with famine; in the day of Thine anger hast Thou slaughtered them; Thou hast not spared. 22He hath called to a solemn day my sojourners round about; and in the day of the LORD’s anger none escaped nor remained, as I prevailed and multiplied all mine enemies.

CHAPTER 3

‘I am the man that seeth poverty by the rod of His wrath upon me. 2He hath taken me, and led me away into darkness, and not into light. 3Surely against me hath He turned His hand all the day. 4My flesh and my skin hath He made old; He hath broken my bones. 5He hath built against me, and compassed mine head, and brought travail upon me. 6He hath set me in dark places, as they that have been long dead. 7He hath built up against me, and I cannot get out; He hath made my chain heavy. 8Also though I cry and shout, He shutteth out my prayer. 9He hath built up my ways; He hath hedged my paths; 10He hath troubled me, as a she-bear lying in wait; He is to me as a lion in secret places. 11He hath pursued me when I fled, and brought me to a stand; He hath made me as one that is put away. 12He hath bent His bow, and set me as a mark for the arrow. 13He hath caused the arrows of His quiver to enter into my reins. 14I was a derision to all my people, and their song all the day. 15He hath filled me with bitterness; He hath made me drunken with gall. 16He hath also broken my teeth with gravel stones; He hath fed me with ashes. 17And He hath removed my soul from peace; I forgat prosperity. 18 19 And I said, “My success is perished, my hope also from the LORD”.I remembered by reason of my poverty, and because of my persecution; my bitterness and gall shall I have in remembrance, 20and my soul shall meditate within me. 21 22 This will I lay up in my heart; therefore will I endure. It is of the LORD’s mercies that He hath not failed me, because His compassions are not at an end. 23They are new every morning; great is Thy faithfulness. 24“The LORD is my portion”, saith my soul; therefore will I wait for Him. 25The LORD is good unto them that wait for Him; the soul that seeketh Him is good, 26and shall await and quietly expect salvation of the LORD. 27It is good for a man when he taketh up a yoke in his youth. 28He will sit alone and keep silence because he hath borne it upon him. 30He will give his cheek to him that smiteth him; he will be filled full with reproaches. 31For the LORD will not cast off for ever. 32For He that hath brought down will have compassion according to the multitude of His mercy. 33He hath not answered from His heart, though He hath brought low the sons of a man. 34To bring down under His feet all the prisoners of the earth, 35to turn aside the judgment of a man before the face of the Most High, 36to condemn a man in his cause, the LORD hath not given commandment. 37Who hath thus spoken, and it hath come to pass? the LORD hath not commanded it. 38Out of the mouth of the LORD shall not proceed evil and good. 39 40 Wherefore should a living man complain, a man concerning his sin? Our way is searched out and tried, and we will turn again unto the LORD. 41Let us lift up our hearts with our hands unto the Most High in the heaven. 42We have transgressed; we have embittered Thee, and Thou hast not pitied. 43Thou hast covered us in anger and persecuted us; Thou hast slain; Thou hast not pitied. 44Thou hast covered Thyself with a cloud, because of prayer. Thou hast set us in the midst of the people to make me to be blind, 45and to be rejected. 46All our enemies have opened their mouth against us. 47Fear and a pit is come upon us, uplifting and destruction. 48Mine eye shall run down with rivers of water, for the destruction of the daughter of my people. 49Mine eye is drowned with tears, and I will not keep silence, so that there shall be no rest, 50till the LORD look down, and behold from heaven. 51Mine eye shall lay my soul bare, because of all the daughters of the city. 52The fowlers chased me sore, like as a sparrow, without a cause; they have cut off my life in a pit, 53and laid a stone upon me. 54Water flowed over mine head; then I said, “I am cut off.” 55I called upon Thy name, O LORD, out of the lowest pit. 56 57 Thou hast heard my voice; close not Thine ears unto my supplication. Thou drewest near unto my help, in the day that I called upon Thee, Thou saidst unto me, “Fear not”. 58O LORD, Thou hast pleaded the causes of my soul; Thou hast redeemed my life. 59O LORD, Thou hast seen my troubles; Thou hast judged my cause. 60Thou hast seen all their vengeance, all their imaginations against me. 61 62 Thou hast heard their reproach, all their imaginations against me; the lips of those that rose up against me, and their devices against me all the day. 63Consider their sitting down, and their rising up; look Thou upon their eyes. 64Thou wilt render unto them a recompence, O LORD, according to the works of their hands. 65Thou wilt recompense them Thine hardship, as a covering of the heart for them. 66Thou wilt persecute them in anger, and consume them from under the heavens, O LORD.

CHAPTER 4

‘How will the gold become dim, the fine silver changed! The stones of the sanctuary are poured out at the top of all the streets. 2The precious sons of Zion that were exalted by gold, how are they esteemed as earthen vessels, the works of the hands of the potter! 3Even the serpents have drawn out the breasts; they give suck to their young ones; the daughters of my people are incurably cruel, like an ostrich in the wilderness. 4The tongue of the sucking child cleaveth to the roof of his mouth for thirst; the little children ask bread, and there is none to break it unto them. 5They that feed upon delicate things are desolate in the streets; they that were nursed in scarlet have covered themselves with dung. 6And the iniquity of the daughter of my people is greater than the iniquities of Sodom that was overthrown as in a moment, and none did labour against her with their hands. 7Her Nazarites were purer than snow; they were whiter than milk; they were purified as with fire; their fragment was of sapphire. 8Their visage was blacker than smoke; they are not known in the streets. Their skin hath cleaved to their bones; they are withered; they are become like a stick. 9They that were slain with the sword were better than they that were slain with hunger; they have departed, stricken through from want of the fruits of the field. 10The hands of the pitiful women have sodden their own children; they became their meat in the destruction of the daughter of my people. 11The LORD hath accomplished His fury; He hath poured out His fierce anger, and hath kindled a fire in Zion; and it hath devoured the foundations thereof. 12The kings of the earth and all the inhabitants of the world did not believe that an enemy and oppressor would enter through the gates of Jerusalem, 13for the sins of her prophets and the iniquities of her priests that have shed the blood of the just in the midst of her. 14Her watchmen wandered in the streets; they were polluted with blood in their weakness; they touched their raiment. 15Depart ye from the unclean; call ye them. Depart, depart, touch them not, for they are on fire; yea, they wander. Say ye among the heathen, “They shall no more sojourn there”. 16The presence of the LORD was their portion, but He will no more regard them. They accepted not the person of the priests; they pitied not the elders. 17While ye yet lived, our eyes failed, while we looked in vain for help. We have looked to a nation that could not save. 18We have hunted for our little ones, that they should not walk in our streets. Our time is drawn near, our days are fulfilled, our time is come. 19Our persecutors were swifter than the eagles of the heaven, they flew upon the mountains, they laid wait for us in the wilderness. 20The breath of our nostrils, the anointed of the LORD, was taken in their destructions, of whom we said, “In His shadow we shall live among the heathen”. 21Rejoice and be glad, O daughter of Edom, that dwellest in the land; the cup also of the LORD shall pass through unto thee; thou shalt be drunken, and shalt pour forth. 22O daughter of Zion, thine iniquity is come to an end; He will no more add to thy captivity; He hath visited thine iniquities, O daughter of Edom; He hath uncovered thy sins.

CHAPTER 5

‘Remember, O LORD, what is come upon us; consider and behold our reproach. 2Our inheritance is turned to strangers, our houses to aliens; 3we are become orphans; there is no father; our mothers are as widows. 4We have drunken our water for money; our wood came by exchange upon our neck. 5We have been persecuted; we have laboured; we have had no rest. 6Egypt gave the hand unto us, Assyria until they were satisfied. 7Our fathers have sinned, and are no more; we have borne their iniquities. 8Servants have ruled over us; there is none to redeem us out of their hand. 9We shall bring in our bread with the peril of our lives because of the sword of the wilderness. 10Our skin is black like an oven; it is dried up from before the storms of famine. 11They humbled the women in Zion, the maids in the cities of Judah. 12Princes were hanged up by their hands; the elders were not honoured. 13The choice men lifted up the millstone, and the young men failed under the wood. 14The elders also have ceased from the gate, the choice men from their music. 15The joy of our heart is ceased; our dance is turned into mourning. 16The crown is fallen from our head: woe unto us, for we have sinned! 17For this, grief is come; our heart is filled with sorrow; for this our eyes are dim. 18For the mountain of Zion, because it is made desolate, foxes have walked upon it. 19But Thou, O LORD, shalt dwell for ever; Thy throne is from generation to generation. 20Wherefore wilt Thou utterly forget us? Wilt Thou forsake us through length of days? 21Turn us unto Thee, O LORD, and we shall be turned, and renew our days as before. 22For Thou hast utterly rejected us; Thou hast been very wroth against us.’
