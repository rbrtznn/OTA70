NAHUM

CHAPTER 1

The burden of Nineveh: the book of the vision of Nahum the Elkoshite. 2God is jealous, and the LORD is a revenger; the LORD revengeth with fury; the LORD taketh vengeance on His adversaries, and He cutteth off His enemies. 3The LORD is slow to anger, and great is His power, and the LORD will not at all acquit the guilty. His way is in destruction and in a whirlwind, and the clouds are the dust of His feet. 4He threateneth the sea, and maketh it dry, and maketh all the rivers desolate; the lands of Bashan and Carmel are diminished, and the flowerings of Lebanon have failed. 5The mountains quake by reason of Him, and the hills are shaken, and the earth is removed from His presence; yea, the world and all that dwell therein. 6Who shall stand before His indignation, and who shall withstand the fierceness of His anger? His fury bringeth kingdoms to naught, and the rocks are burst asunder by Him. 7The LORD is good to them that wait upon Him in the day of affliction, and He knoweth them that reverence Him. 8But with an overrunning flood He will make an utter end of them that rise up against Him; darkness shall pursue His enemies. 9What do ye imagine against the LORD? He will make an utter end; He will not take vengeance twice for the same thing with affliction. 10For the enemy shall be laid bare even unto the foundation, and shall be consumed as a twisted yew, and as stubble fully dry. 11There shall a device come out of thee against the LORD counseling evil things, things that are contrary. 12Thus saith the LORD that ruleth over many waters, ‘Even thus shall they be sent away, and the report of thee shall be heard no more. 13And now will I break his rod from off thee and will burst thy bonds.’ 14And the LORD shall give a commandment concerning thee: ‘There shall no more of thy name be scattered; out of the house of thy god will I cut off the graven images and the molten images; I will make thy grave, for they are fleeting’.

CHAPTER 2

Behold upon the mountains the feet of Him that bringeth glad tidings and publisheth peace! O Judah, keep thy feasts; perform thy vows, for they shall no more pass through thee to 2 their decay. It is finished with him; he hath been removed; one that hath been delivered from affliction is come up breathing into thy presence. Watch the way, strengthen thy loins, be very valiant in thy strength; 3for the LORD hath turned aside the pride of Jacob, as He did the pride of Israel. For shaking they have shaken them off, and destroyed their vine branches. 4They destroyed the weapons of their power from men, mighty men, sporting with fire. The reins of their chariots in the day of His preparation, and the horsemen shall be thrown into confusion 5in the streets, and the chariots shall jostle one against another, and be caught in each other in the broad ways. Their appearance is as lamps of fire, and as gleaming lightnings. 6And their mighty men shall remember and flee by day, and they shall be weak as they go, and they shall make haste to their walls, and prepare their defences. 7The gates of the rivers are opened, and the palaces have fallen into ruin, 8and the foundation hath been discovered; and she hath gone up, and her maidservants were led away as doves mourning in their hearts. 9And as for Nineveh, her waters are as a pool of water, and they fled away and stayed not, and there was none that would look back. 10They took the spoil of silver, they took the spoil of gold, and there was none end of their adorning; they have been laden upon all their desirable vessels. 11There is shaking and quaking and trembling, and breaking of heart, and loosing of knees, and pains in all loins, and the faces of them all are as the blackening of a pot. 12Where is the dwelling of the lions, and the feeding place of the young lions, where the lion walked to enter in; a young lion, and there was none to scare him away? 13The lion did seize enough for his whelps, and strangled for his lions, and filled his hole with game, and his dwelling place with prey. 14‘Behold, I am against thee’, saith the LORD Almighty, ‘and I will burn thy multitude in the smoke, and the sword shall devour thy lions, and I will cut off thy prey from the land, and thy works shall no more be heard.”

CHAPTER 3

O city of blood, wholly false, full of unrighteousness; the prey shall not be touched. 2A noise of whips, and a noise of the rattling of the wheels, and of the pursuing horse, and of the jumping chariot, and of the mounted horseman, and of the bright sword, and of the glittering weapons, and of a multitude of wounded, and of heavy falling. And there was no end to her nations, but they shall be weak in their bodies, 4because of the multitude of whoredoms. She is a fair harlot and well favoured, skilled in witchcrafts, that selleth the nations through her whoredoms and peoples through her witchcrafts. 5‘Behold, I am against thee’, saith the LORD God Almighty, ‘and I will discover thy hinder parts before thy face, and I will shew the nations thy shame and the kingdoms thy dishonour. 6And I will cast abominable filth upon thee according to thine unclean ways, and will set thee as an example. 7And it shall come to pass that every one that looketh upon thee shall go down from thee, and say, ‘Wretched Nineveh! Who will bemoan her? Whence shall I seek comfort for her?’ 8Prepare thee a portion, tune a chord, prepare a portion for Amon; she that dwelleth among the rivers, water is round about her whose dominion is the sea, and her walls are water. 9And Ethiopia is her strength, and Egypt, and there is no end to her flight; even the Libyans are become her helpers. 10Yet shall she go as a prisoner into captivity; her young children also shall they dash in pieces to the ground at the top of all her streets. And they shall cast lots for all her glorious things, and all her great men shall be bound in chains. 11Thou also shalt be drunken, and shalt be despised; thou also shalt seek thee a place of strength from thine enemies. 12All thy strongholds are like fig trees having watchers; if they be shaken, they shall even fall into the mouth of the eater. Behold, thy people in the midst of thee are as women; the gates of thy land shall be opened to thine enemies; the fire shall devour thy bars. 14Draw thee water for a siege; fortify thy strongholds; go into the clay, and let it be trodden in the chaff; make it stronger than brick. 15There shall the fire devour thee; the sword shall cut thee off; it shall eat thee up as the cankerworm, and thou shalt be pressed down like the palmerworm. 16Thou hast multiplied thy merchandise beyond the stars of heaven; the palmerworm hath attacked and flown away. 17Thy commingled multitude is suddenly departed as the grasshopper, as the locust that hath sat in the hedge in a frosty day. The sun ariseth, and it flieth away, and knoweth not its place. Woe unto them! 18Thy shepherds have slumbered; the king of Assyria hath laid low thy mighty men; thy people are gone away unto the mountains, and there was none that would receive them. 19There is no healing of thy bruise; thy wound hath rankled. All that hear the report of thee shall clap the hands over thee, for upon whom hath not thy wickedness passed continually?
