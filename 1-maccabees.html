1 MACCABEES

CHAPTER 1

And it happened, after that Alexander son of Philip, the Macedonian, who came out of the land of Kittim, had smitten Darius king of the Persians and Medes, that he reigned in his stead, being before king over Greece. 2And he made many wars, and won many strongholds, and slaughtered the kings of the earth. 3And he went through to the ends of the earth, and took spoils of many nations. And the earth was quiet before him; and he was exalted, and his heart was lifted up. 4And he gathered a mighty strong host, and ruled over countries, and nations, and kings; and they became tributaries unto him. 5And after these things, he fell upon his bed, and perceived that he should die. 6And he called his servants, such as were honourable and had been brought up with him from his youth, and parted his kingdom among them while he was yet alive. 7So Alexander reigned twelve years, and then died. 8And his servants bare rule, every one in his place. 9And after his death they all put crowns upon themselves, and their sons after them many years; and they multiplied evils in the earth. 10And there came out of them a sinful root, Antiochus surnamed Epiphanes, son of Antiochus the king, who had been hostage at Rome; and he became king in the hundred and thirty and seventh year of the kingdom of the Greeks.

11In those days went there out of Israel sons that transgressed the law, and persuaded many, saying, ‘Let us go and make a covenant with the nations that are round about us; for since we have departed from them, many evils have found us’. 12 13 And this device found favour in their eyes. So certain of the people were desirous to be forward herein; and they went to the king, and he gave them licence to do after the ordinances of the Gentiles. 14Whereupon they built a gymnasium in Jerusalem according to the customs of the nations, 15and they made themselves uncircumcised, and forsook the holy covenant, and joined themselves to the nations, and sold themselves to do evil.

16And when the kingdom was established before Antiochus, he thought to reign over the land of Egypt, that he might have dominion of the two realms. 17Wherefore he entered into Egypt with a great multitude, with chariots, and elephants, and horsemen, and a great navy, 18and made war against Ptolemy king of Egypt. And Ptolemy turned from before him and fled, and many were wounded and fell. 19And they took the strong cities in the land of Egypt, and he took the spoils of the land of Egypt. 20And after that Antiochus had smitten Egypt, he returned again in the hundred forty and third year, and went up against Israel and Jerusalem with a great multitude. 21And he entered into the sanctuary is his great pride, and took away the golden altar, and the lampstand of light, and all the vessels thereof, 22and the table of presentation, and the libation vessels, and the vials, and the censers of gold, and the veil, and the crowns; and all the golden ornament that was on the face of the temple he pulled off. 23He took also the silver and the gold, and the precious vessels; also he took the hidden treasures which he found. 24And taking all these things away, he went into his own land, and made deeds of murder, and spake very proudly. 25And there was great mourning in Israel, in every place where they were; 26and the princes and elders groaned, and the virgins and young men were made feeble, and the beauty of the women was changed. 27Every bridegroom took up lamentation, and she that sat in the marriage chamber was in mourning. 28The land also was moved for the inhabitants thereof, and all the house of Jacob was clothed with shame.

29And after two years the king sent his chief collector of tribute unto the cities of Judah; and he came into Jerusalem with a great host. 30And he spake peaceable words unto them with deceit, and they gave him credence. But he fell suddenly upon the city, and smote it with a great blow, and destroyed much people of Israel. 31And he took the spoils of the city, and set it on fire, and pulled down the houses and walls thereof on every side. 32And the women and the children took they captive, and possessed the cattle. 33Then builded they the city of David with a great and strong wall, and with mighty towers; and it became a stronghold for them. 34And they put therein a sinful nation, transgressors, and they made themselves strong therein. 35They laid up also arms and victuals; and gathering together the spoil of Jerusalem, they laid it up there; and they became a sore menace. 36It became a place to lie in wait against the sanctuary, and an evil adversary to Israel continually. 37And they shed innocent blood on every side of the temple, and defiled the sanctuary. 38And the inhabitants of Jerusalem fled because of them; and she was made a habitation of Gentiles, and became stranger to those that were born in her; and her own children forsook her. 39Her temple was laid waste like a wilderness; her feasts were turned into mourning, her Sabbaths into a disgrace, her honour into contempt. 40As had been her glory, so was her dishonour increased, and her exaltation was turned into mourning.

41And the king wrote to his whole kingdom, that all should be as one people, 42and every one should leave his laws; and all the nations agreed according to the commandment of the king. 43Yea, many also of Israel consented to his service, and sacrificed unto idols, and profaned the Sabbath. 44And the king sent letters by the hand of messengers unto Jerusalem and the cities of Judah, that they should follow precepts that were strange to the land, 45and withhold whole burnt-offerings and sacrifice and drink offerings in the temple; and that they should profane the Sabbaths and festival days, 46and defile the holy precinct and the holy people; 47to set up altars, and groves, and temples of idols, and sacrifice swine’s flesh and common beasts; 48that they should also leave their sons uncircumcised, and make their souls abominable with all manner of uncleanness and profanation; 49to the end that they might forget the law, and change all the ordinances. 50And whosoever would not do according to the commandment of the king, he should die. 51According to all these words wrote he to his whole kingdom, and appointed overseers over all the people, commanding the cities of Judah to sacrifice city by city. 52Then many of the people joined themselves to them, to wit, every one that forsook the law; and so they committed evils in the land. 53And they forced Israel into secret places, even in every place of refuge that they had. 54Now the fifteenth day of the month Chislev, in the hundred forty and fifth year, they set up the abomination of desolation upon the altar, and builded altars in the cities of Judah on every side, 55and burnt incense at the doors of the houses, and in the streets. 56And they rent in pieces the books of the law which they found, and burnt them with fire. 57And wheresoever was found one with any book of the covenant, or if any consented to the law, the king’s judgment put him to death. 58They did by their might unto them that were found in Israel month by month in the cities. 59Now the five and twentieth day of the month they did sacrifice upon the altar, which was upon the altar of whole burnt-offering. 60And according to the commandment they put to death the women that had caused their children to be circumcised. 61And they hanged the infants about their necks, and slew their families and them that had circumcised them. 62Howbeit many in Israel were strong, and confirmed in themselves not to eat any unclean thing. 63Wherefore they chose rather to die, that they might not be defiled with meats, and that they might not profane the holy covenant; so then they died. 64And there was very great wrath upon Israel.

CHAPTER 2

In those days arose Mattathias the son of John, the son of Simeon, a priest of the sons of Joarib, and left Jerusalem, and dwelt in Modein. 2And he had five sons: John, who was called Gaddi, 3Simon, who was called Thassi, 4Judas, who was called Maccabeus, 5Eleazar, who was called Avaran, and Jonathan, who was called Apphus. 6And he saw the blasphemies that were committed in Judah and Jerusalem, 7and said, ‘Woe is me! Wherefore was I born to see this, the ruin of my people, and the destruction of the holy city, and to dwell there when it was delivered into the hand of the enemy, and the sanctuary into the hand of Gentiles? 8Her temple is become as a man without honour; 9her glorious vessels were carried away into captivity; her infants were slain in the streets, her young men with the sword of the enemy. 10What nation hath not had a part in her kingdom, and gotten of her spoils? 11All her ornaments were taken away; of a free woman she is become a bondslave. 12And behold, our sanctuary, our beauty and our glory, is laid waste, and the Gentiles have defiled them. 13To what end therefore shall we live any longer?’ 14Then Mattathias and his sons rent their clothes, and put on sackcloth, and mourned very sore.

15And the the king’s officers, such as compelled the apostasy, came into the city Modein to make them sacrifice. 16And many of Israel came unto them; and Mattathias and his sons gathered together. 17Then answered the king’s officers and said to Mattathias, saying, ‘Thou art a ruler and a glorious and great man in this city, and supported by sons and brethren. 18Now therefore, come thou first and fulfil the king’s commandment, like as all the Gentiles have done, yea, and the men of Judah also and such as remain at Jerusalem; so shalt thou and thy house be among the friends of the king; and thou and thy sons shall be glorified with silver and gold and many rewards.’ 19Then Mattathias answered and spake with a loud voice, ‘Though all the Gentiles that are in the house of the king’s dominion obey him, and fall away every one from the religion of their fathers, and give consent to his commandments, 20yet will I and my sons and my brethren walk in the covenant of our fathers. 21God forbid that we should forsake the law and the ordinances. 22We will not obey the king’s words, nor turn aside from our religion either on the right hand or the left.’ 23Now when he had left speaking these words, there came out one of the Judeans in the sight of all, to sacrifice on the altar at Modein, according to the king’s commandment. 24Which thing Mattathias saw, and he was inflamed with zeal, and his reins trembled, and his anger arose in judgment; wherefore he ran and slew him upon the altar. 25Also the king’s commissioner, who compelled men to sacrifice at that time, he killed, and the altar he pulled down. 26 27 Thus had he zeal for the law, like as Phinehas did unto Zimri son of Salu. And Mattathias cried throughout the city with a loud voice, saying, ‘Whosoever is zealous of the law, and upholdeth the covenant, let him follow me’. 28And he and his sons fled into the mountains, and abandoned all that they had in the city.

29At that time many that sought after justice and judgment went down into the wilderness to dwell there, 30both they, and their children, and their wives, and their cattle, because evils increased sore upon them. 31Now it was announced to the king’s men and the host that was at Jerusalem, the city of David, that the men who had spurned the king’s commandment were gone down into the secret places of the wilderness. 32Wherefore pursued after them a great number, and having overtaken them, they camped against them, and made war against them on the Sabbath day. 33And they said unto them, ‘Enough; come forth, and do according to the commandment of the king, and ye shall live’. 34But they said, ‘We will not come forth, neither will we do the king’s bidding, and profane the Sabbath day’. 35So then they gave them battle with all speed. 36Howbeit they answered them not, neither cast they a stone at them, nor stopped the places where they lay hid, 37but said, ‘Let us die all in our innocency; heaven and earth bear witness for us that ye put us to death unjustly’. 38So they rose up against them in battle on the Sabbath; and they died with their wives and children and their cattle, as many as a thousand souls.

39Now Mattathias and his friends understood hereof, and they mourned for them right sore. 40And they said, a man to his neighbour, ‘If we all do as our brethren have done, and fight not for our lives and ordinances against the Gentiles, they will now quickly root us out of the earth’. 41At that time therefore they decreed, saying, ‘Whosoever shall come to make battle with us on the Sabbath day, let us fight against him; neither will we die all, as our brethren died in the secret places’. 42Then joined there unto them a gathering of Hasideans, a mighty host of Israel, even all such as voluntarily offered themselves unto the law. 43Also every one that escaped from the evils was added unto them, and became a stay unto them. 44So they gathered a force together, and smote sinful men in their anger, and transgressors in their wrath; and the rest fled to the Gentiles to be saved. 45Then Mattathias and his friends went round about, and pulled down the altars; 46and all the boys they found within the borders of Israel uncircumcised, those they circumcised by force. 47Thus they pursued also after the sons of insolence, and the work prospered in their hand. 48So they recovered the law out of the hand of the Gentiles, and out of the hand of kings; neither suffered they the sinner to triumph over them.

49Now the time drew near that Mattathias should die, and he said unto his sons, ‘Now have pride and rebuke gotten strength, and the time of destruction, and the wrath of indignation. 50Now therefore, my sons, be ye zealous for the law, and give your lives for the covenant of our fathers. 51Call to remembrance what acts our fathers did in their generations, and receive great honour and an everlasting name. 52Was not Abraham found faithful in temptation, and it was imputed to him for righteousness? 53Joseph in the time of his distress kept the commandment, and became LORD of Egypt. 54Phinehas our father, in being zealous and fervent, received a covenant of an everlasting priesthood. 55Joshua, for fulfiling the word, became a judge in Israel. 56Caleb, for bearing witness before the congregation, received an inheritance of the land. 57David in his mercy inherited the throne of the kingdom for ever. 58Elijah, for being zealous and fervent for the law, was taken up into heaven. 59Ananias, Azarias and Misael, by believing, were saved out of the flame. 60Daniel, for his innocency, was delivered from the mouth of lions. 61And thus consider ye from generation to generation that all that put their trust in Him shall not be weakened. 62Fear not then the words of a sinful man, for his glory shall be dung and worms. 63Today he shall be lifted up, and tomorrow he shall not be found, because he is returned into his dust, and his counsels shall come to nothing. 64Wherefore, ye my children, be valiant and strong men in the behalf of the law, for by it shall ye obtain glory. 65And behold, your brother Simeon; I know that he is a man of counsel; give ear unto him always; he shall be a father unto you. 66As for Judah Maccabeus, he hath been strong in power even from his youth up; he shall be your captain, and fight the battle of the people. 67And ye shall take unto you all those that observe the law, and do vengeance for your people. 68Recompense fully the Gentiles, and take heed to the ordinance of the law.’ 69So he blessed them, and was added to his fathers. 70And he died in the hundred forty and sixth year, and was buried in the sepulchres of his fathers at Modein; and all Israel mourned him with great mourning.

CHAPTER 3

Then his son Judas, called Maccabeus, rose up in his stead. 2And all his brethren helped him, and so did all they that held with his father, and they fought with cheerfulness the battle of Israel. 3So he increased the glory of his people, and put on a breastplate as a giant, and girt his warlike harness about him; and he made battles, protecting the camp with his sword. 4In his acts he was like a lion, and like a lion’s whelp roaring for his prey. 5For he sought out the transgressors, and burnt up those that vexed his people. 6Wherefore the transgressors shrunk for fear of him, and all the workers of transgression were troubled, and salvation prospered in his hand. 7He grieved also many kings, and made Jacob glad with his acts; and his memorial shall be a blessing for ever. 8Moreover he went through the cities of Judah, destroying the ungodly out of them, and turning away wrath from Israel. 9And his name was known unto the utmost part of the earth, and he gathered together such as were perishing.

10Then Apollonius gathered the nations together, and a great host out of Samaria, to fight against Israel. 11And Judas perceived this, and went forth to meet him; and he smote him, and slew him. Many also fell down wounded, and the rest fled. 12And they took their spoils, and Judas took Apollonius’ sword, and therewith he fought all his days. 13Now Seron, the leader of the army of Syria, heard say that Judas had gathered unto him a multitude and company of the faithful that went out with him to war. 14And he said, ‘I will get me a name, and be glorified in the kingdom, and I will go fight with Judas, and them that are with him, who despise the king’s word’. 15And there gathered together, and went up with him, a mighty host of the ungodly, to help him, and to be avenged of the people of Israel. 16And he came near to the going up of Beth-Horon; and Judas went forth to meet him with a small company; 17who, when they saw the host coming to meet them, said unto Judas, ‘How shall we be able, being so few, to fight against so great a multitude, and so strong, seeing we are ready to faint with fasting all this day?’ 18Unto whom Judas answered, ‘It is no hard matter for many to be shut up in the hands of a few, and before heaven it is all one to deliver by many or few. 19For victory in battle standeth not in the multitude of a host, but strength cometh from heaven. 20They come against us in much pride and iniquity to destroy us, and our wives and children, and to spoil us; 21but we fight for our lives and our laws. 22And He Himself will crush them before our face; and as for you, be ye not afraid of them.’ 23Now as soon as he had left off speaking, he drave suddenly upon them; and Seron and his host were crushed before him. 24And they pursued them from the going down of Beth- Horon unto the plain; and there fell about eight hundred men of them; and the residue fled into the land of the Philistines. 25Then began the fear of Judas and his brethren, and exceeding great dread fell upon the nations round about them. 26And his fame reached unto the king, and all nations talked of the battles of Judas.

27Now when king Antiochus heard these things, he was full of indignation, and sent and gathered together all the forces of his realm, even a very strong army. 28He opened also his treasure, and gave his soldiers pay for a year, commanding them to be ready whensoever they should be needed. 29And he saw that the money of his treasuries failed, and that the tributes in the country were small, because of the dissension and the affliction which he had brought upon the land, in taking away the laws which had been of old time. 30And he feared that he should not have enough to bear his charges any longer, and for the gifts that he was used to give so liberally as before, wherein he had abounded above the kings that were before him. 31Wherefore, being greatly perplexed in his mind, he determined to go into Persia, there to take the tribute of those countries, and to gather much money. 32And he left Lysias, a man of distinction, one of the blood royal, over the affairs of the king from the river Euphrates unto the borders of Egypt, 33and to bring up his son Antiochus until he came again. 34Moreover he delivered unto him the half of his forces, and the elephants, and gave him charge of all things that he would have done, as also concerning them that dwelt in Judah and Jerusalem; 35that he should send an army against them, to destroy and root out the strength of Israel and the remnant of Jerusalem, and to take away their memorial from that place, 36and that he should place Gentiles in all their quarters, and divide their land by lot. 37So the king took half of the forces that remained, and departed from Antioch, the capital city of his kingdom, the hundred forty and seventh year. And he passed the river Euphrates, and went through the high countries.

38Then Lysias chose Ptolemy the son of Dorymenes, and Nicanor and Gorgias, mighty men, friends of the king. 39And with them he sent forty thousand footmen and seven thousand horsemen, to go into the land of Judah, and to destroy it according to the king’s command. 40So he went forth with all their power, and they came and pitched by Emmaus in the plain country. 41And the merchants of the country, hearing the fame of them, took silver and gold very much with shackles; and they came into the camp to buy the children of Israel for slaves. And unto them there were added a power also of Syria and of the land of the Philistines. 42Now when Judas and his brethren saw that the evils were multiplied, and that the forces did encamp themselves in their lands, and learned of the commandment of the king, which he had given to destroy the people, and utterly abolish them, 43they said each to his neighbour, ‘Let us rise up against the decayed estate of our people, and let us fight for our people and the sanctuary’. 44Then the congregation gathered together, that they might be ready for battle, and that they might pray and ask mercy and compassion. 45Now Jerusalem was uninhabited like a wilderness; there was none of her children went in or out. The sanctuary also was trodden down, and the sons of strangers were in the stronghold, a dwelling-place for the nations, and joy was taken from Jacob, and the pipe with the harp ceased. 46Wherefore they assembled themselves together, and came to Mizpah over against Jerusalem; for Mizpah was a place of prayer aforetime in Israel. 47Then they fasted that day, and put on sackcloth, and cast ashes upon their heads, and rent their clothes, 48and laid open the book of the law, seeking that concerning which the nations inquire of the images of their idols. 49They brought also the priest’s garments, and the first-fruits and the tithes, and the Nazarites they stirred up, who had accomplished their days. 50Then cried they with a loud voice toward heaven, saying, ‘What shall we do with these, and whither shall we carry them away? 51For Thy sanctuary is trodden down and profaned, and Thy priests are in mourning and humiliation. 52And, lo, the nations are assembled together against us to destroy us; what things they imagine against us, Thou knowest. 53How shall we be able to stand against them, except Thou be our help?’ 54Then sounded they with the trumpets, and cried with a loud voice. 55And after this Judas ordained captains over the people, even captains over thousands, and captains over hundreds, and captains over fifties, and captains over tens. 56But as for such as were building houses, or were betrothed to women, or were planting vineyards, or were fearful, those he commanded that they should return every man to his own house, according to the law. 57So the company removed, and pitched upon the south side of Emmaus. 58And Judas said, ‘Gird yourselves, and be valiant sons, and be in readiness against the morning, that ye may fight with these nations that are assembled together against us to destroy us, and our sanctuary. 59For it is better for us to die in battle than to behold the evils of our nation and our sanctuary. 60Nevertheless, as the will of heaven is, so shall He do.’

CHAPTER 4

Then took Gorgias five thousand footmen, and a thousand of the select horsemen; and the company removed by night, 2to the end that they might fall upon the camp of the Judeans, and smite them suddenly. And the sons of the fortress were his guides. 3Now Judas heard thereof; and he himself went out, and the valiant men with him, that he might smite the king’s army which was at Emmaus, 4 5 while as yet the forces were dispersed from the camp. And Gorgias came by night to the camp of Judas, and found no man, and sought them in the mountains; for he said, ‘These fellows flee from us’. 6But as soon as it was day, Judah was seen in the plain with three thousand men, who nevertheless had neither armour nor swords such as they desired. 7And they saw the camp of the nations, that it was strong and well defended, and compassed round about with horsemen, and these were instructed unto war. 8Then said Judas to the men that were with him, ‘Fear ye not their multitude, neither be ye afraid of their assault. 9Remember how that our fathers were delivered in the Red Sea, when Pharoah pursued them with force. 10Now therefore let us cry unto heaven, if peradventure He will be with us, and remember the covenant of our fathers, and destroy this host before our face this day. 11Then all the nations shall know that there is One Who delivereth and saveth Israel.’ 12Then the Gentiles lifted up their eyes, and saw them coming over against them. 13Wherefore they went out of the camp to battle, but they that were with Judas sounded their trumpets. 14and joined battle with them, and the Gentiles were crushed, and fled into the plain. 15Howbeit all the hindermost of them fell by the sword; and they pursued them unto Gazera, and unto the plains of Edom and Ashdod and Jamnia, and of them there fell three thousand men. 16Then Judas returned again with his host from pursuing them, 17and said to the people, ‘Be not desirous of the spoils, inasmuch as there is a battle before us, 18and Gorgias and his host are near unto us in the mountain. But stand ye now against our enemies and destroy them, and after this boldly take the spoils.’ 19As Judas was yet speaking these words, there appeared a detachment looking out of the mountain; 20and they perceived that they had been put to flight, and that the Judeans were burning the tents, for the smoke that was seen declared what was done. 21When therefore they perceived these things, they were sore afraid, and seeing also the host of Judas in the plain ready to fight, 22they fled every one into the land of the Philistines. 23Then Judas returned to spoil the tents, and they got much gold and silver, and cloth of blue and purple of the sea, and great riches. 24And they returned, and sang hymns and blessed heaven, because He is good, because His mercy endureth for ever. 25And Israel had a great deliverance that day.

26Now as many of the Philistines that had escaped went and told Lysias all that had happened; 27who, when he heard thereof, was confounded and discouraged, because neither such things as he would were done unto Israel, nor such things as the king commanded him were come to pass. 28And in the following year he gathered together threescore thousand choice men, and five thousand horsemen, that he might make war on them. 29So they came into Edom, and pitched their tents at Beth-Zur, and Judas met them with ten thousand men. 30And he saw that mighty army, and he prayed and said, ‘Blessed art Thou, O Saviour of Israel, Who didst crush the attack of the mighty man by the hand of Thy servant David, and deliveredst the camp of the Philistines into the hands of Jonathan the son of Saul, and his armourbearer. 31Shut up this army in the hand of Thy people Israel, and let them be put to shame in their army and horsemen. 32Make them to be of no courage, and melt the boldness of their strength, and let them be shaken in their destruction. 33Cast them down with the sword of them that love Thee, and let all those that know Thy name praise Thee.’ 34So they joined battle; and there fell of the host of Lysias about five thousand men, and even before them did they fall. 35Now when Lysias saw his army put to flight, and the manliness of Judas’ soldiers, and how they were ready either to live or die valiantly, he retired into Antioch, and gathered together mercenaries; and his army having become great again, that he might come again into Judah.

36Then said Judas and his brethren, ‘Behold, our enemies are discomfited; let us go up to cleanse and rededicate the sanctuary’. 37And all the host assembled themselves together, and went up into mount Zion. 38And they saw the sanctuary desolate, and the altar profaned, and the gates burned up, and shrubs growing in the court, as in a forest or in one of the mountains, yea, and the priests’ chambers pulled down. 39And they rent their clothes, and made great lamentation, and cast ashes upon their heads, 40and fell down flat to the ground upon their faces, and blew an alarm with their trumpets, and cried toward heaven. 41Then Judas appointed men to fight against those that were in the fortress, until he could cleanse the sanctuary. 42So he chose priests of blameless conduct, such as were devoted to the law, 43and they cleansed the sanctuary, and took away the stones of defilement into an unclean place. 44And they consulted concerning the altar of whole burnt- offerings, which was defiled, touching what they should do with it. 45And there fell to them a good counsel, to pull it down, that it should not be a reproach to them because the Gentiles had defiled it. Wherefore they pulled down the altar, 46and laid up the stones in the mountain of the temple in a convenient place, until there should come a prophet to give answer concerning them. 47Then they took whole new stones according to the law, and built a new altar according to the former. 48And they built up the sanctuary and the inner courts of the temple, and hallowed the courts. 49They made also new holy vessels, and into the temple they brought the lampstand, and the altar of incense, and the table. 50And upon the altar they burned incense, and the lamps that were upon the altar they lighted, and they gave light in the temple. 51Furthermore they set the loaves upon the table, and spread out the veils, and finished all the works which they had made. 52Now on the five and twentieth day of the ninth month, which is called the month Chislev, in the hundred forty and eighth year, they rose up early in the morning, 53and offered sacrifice according to the law upon the new altar of whole burnt-offerings which they had made. 54At what time and day the nations had profaned it, even in that day was it dedicated with songs, and stringed instruments, and harps and cymbals. 55Then all the people fell upon their faces, and worshipped and blessed heaven, which had given them good success. 56And so they accomplished the dedication of the altar in eight days, and they offered whole burnt-offerings with gladness, and sacrificed the sacrifice of deliverance and praise. 57They decked also the forefront of the temple with crowns of gold, and with shields; and the gates and the priests’ chambers they renewed, and hanged doors upon them. 58Then was there very great gladness among the people, and the reproach of the Gentiles was put away. 59Moreover Judas and his brethren with the whole congregation of Israel ordained that the days of rededication of the altar should be kept in their season from year to year by the space of eight days, from the five and twentieth day of the month Chislev, with joy and gladness. 60At that time also they built up the mount Zion round about with high walls and strong towers, that the nations should not return and tread them down, as they had done before. 61And Judas set there a garrison to defend it, and he fortified it to defend Beth-Zur, that the people might have a fortress over against Edom.

CHAPTER 5

Now when the nations round about heard that the altar was built, and the sanctuary renewed as before, they were angered very greatly. 2Wherefore they thought to destroy the race of Jacob that was among them, and thereupon they began to slay and destroy the people. 3Then Judas fought against the sons of Esau in Edom at Acrabattine, because they besieged Israel; and he struck them with a great overthrow, and reduced them, and took their spoils. 4Also he remembered the injury of the children of Baean, who had been a snare and offence unto the people, in that they lay in wait for them in the ways. 5He shut them up therefore in the towers, and encamped against them, and anathematized them, and burned their towers with fire, together with all that were therein. 6And he passed over to the children of Ammon, and he found a mighty power and much people with Timothy, their captain. 7So he fought many battles with them, and they were discomfited before him, and he defeated them. 8And he overpowered Jazer and the villages belonging thereto, and returned into Judah.

9Then the nations that were at Gilead assembled themselves together against the Israelites that were in their quarters to destroy them, and they fled into the fortress of Dathema 10and sent letters unto Judas and his brethren, saying, ‘The Gentiles that are round about us are assembled together against us to destroy us, 11and they are preparing to come and take the fortress wherein we have fled for refuge, Timothy being the captain of their host. 12Come now therefore, and deliver us from their hands, for many of us are slain, 13yea, all our brethren that were in the places of Tob are put to death. Their wives and their children also they have carried away captives, and borne away their stuff, and they have destroyed there about a thousand men.’ 14And while these letters were yet being read, behold, there came other messengers from Galilee with their clothes rent, who reported on this wise 15and said, ‘They of Ptolemais, and of Tyre and Sidon, and all Galilee of the nations, are assembled together against us to consume us’. 16Now when Judas and the people heard these words, there assembled a great congregation together, to consult what they should do for their brethren that were in trouble and assaulted of them. 17Then said Judas unto Simon his brother, ‘Choose thee out men, and go and deliver thy brethren that are in Galilee; 23And it came to pass after the death of Judas that the transgressors came forth in all the land of Israel, and there arose up all such as wrought injustice. 24In those days also was there a very great famine, and the country revolted and went with them. 25Then Bacchides chose ungodly men, and appointed them lords of the country. 26And they made enquiry and search for Judah’ friends, and brought them unto Bacchides, who took vengeance of them, and used them despitefully. 27So was there a great affliction in Israel, the like whereof was not since the time that a prophet was not seen among them. 28And all Judas’ friends came together, and said unto Jonathan, 29‘Since thy brother Judas has died, there is no man like him to go forth against our enemies and Bacchides, and against them of our nation that are adversaries to us. 30Now therefore, we have chosen thee this day to be our prince and captain in his stead, that thou mayest fight our battles.’ 31And Jonathan took the governance upon him at that time, and rose up in the stead of his brother Judas.

32But when Bacchides got knowledge thereof, he sought for to slay him. 33Then Jonathan, and Simon his brother, and all that were with him, perceiving, fled into the wilderness of Tekoa, and pitched their tents by the water of the pool Asphar. 34Which when Bacchides understood, he came near to Jordan with all his host upon the Sabbath day. 35Now Jonathan had sent his brother John, a captain of the host, and prayed his friends the Nebathites that they might leave with them their carriage, which was much. 36But the children of Jambri came out of Medaba and took John, and all that he had, and having them, went their way. 37After this came word to Jonathan and Simon his brother that the children of Jambri made a great marriage, and were bringing the bride from Nadabath with a great train, as being the daughter of one of the great princes of Canaan. 38Therefore they remembered the blood of John their brother, and went up, and hid themselves under the covert of the mountain. 39And they lifted up their eyes, and looked; and behold, there was much ado and great carriage; and the bridegroom came forth, and his friends and brethren, to meet them with drums, and instruments of music, and many weapons. 40Then they rose up against them from the place where they lay in ambush, and made a slaughter of them; and many that were wounded fell down, and the remnant fled into the mountain, and they took all their spoils. 41Thus was the marriage turned into mourning, and the noise of their melody into lamentation. 42So they avenged fully the blood of their brother, and turned again to the marsh of Jordan. 43Now Bacchides heard thereof, and came on the Sabbath day unto the banks of Jordan with a great power. 44Then Jonathan said to his company, ‘Let us go up now and fight for our lives, for it standeth not today as in time past. 45For behold, the battle is before us and behind us, and the water of Jordan on this side and that side, the marsh likewise and wood, neither is there any place for us to turn aside. 46Wherefore cry ye now unto heaven, that ye may be delivered from the hand of your enemies.’ 47And the battle was joined, and Jonathan stretched forth his hand to smite Bacchides, but he turned back from him toward the rear. 48Then Jonathan and they that were with him leapt into Jordan, and swam over unto the farther bank; howbeit the other passed not over Jordan unto them. 49So there fell with Bacchides that day up to a thousand men. 50And Bacchides returned to Jerusalem, and built strong cities in Judah: the fort in Jericho, and Emmaus, and Beth-Horon, and Bethel, and Timnath, Purahthon and Tephon, with high walls, with gates and with bars. 51And in them he set garrisons, that they might work malice upon Israel. 52He fortified also the city Beth-Zur, and Gazara, and the tower, and put forces in them, and provision of victuals. 53Besides, he took the chief men’s sons in the country for hostages, and put them into the tower at Jerusalem to be kept.

54Moreover in the hundred fifty and third year, in the second month, Alcimus commanded that the wall of the inner court of the sanctuary should be pulled down. He pulled down also the works of the prophets. 55And he began to pull it down; but even at that time was Alcimus plagued, and his enterprises hindered. For his mouth was stopped, and he was taken with a palsy, so that he could no more speak any thing nor give order concerning his house. 56So Alcimus died at that time with great torment. 57Now Bacchides saw that Alcimus was dead, and he returned to the king, and the land of Judah was in rest two years.

58Then all the ungodly men held a council, saying, ‘Behold, Jonathan and his company are at ease and dwell without care; now therefore we will bring Bacchides hither, who shall take them all in one night’. 59So they went and consulted with him. 60Then removed he, and came with a great host, and sent letters privily to his adherents in Judah, that they should take Jonathan and those that were with him; howbeit they could not, because their counsel was known unto them. 61Wherefore Jonathan and his men took of the men of the country that were leaders of that mischief, about fifty persons, and slew them. 62And Jonathan and Simon and they that were with him, got them away to Bethbasi, which is in the wilderness, and they repaired the decays thereof and made it strong. 63Which thing when Bacchides knew, he gathered together all his host, and sent word to them that were of Judah. 64Then went he and pitched camp against Bethbasi, and they fought against it a long season, and made engines of war. 65But Jonathan left his brother Simon in the city, and went forth himself into the country, and with a certain number went he forth. 66And he smote Odomera, and his brethren, and the children of Phasiron, in their tent. 67And he began to smite them, and came up with the forces. And Simon and his company went out of the city, and burned up the engines of war, 68and fought against Bacchides; and he was discomfited by them, and they afflicted him sore, for his counsel and attack was in vain. 69Wherefore he was very wroth at the transgressors that gave him counsel to come into the country, insomuch as he slew many of them, and purposed to return into his own country. 70Whereof when Jonathan had knowledge, he sent ambassadors unto him, to the end that he should make peace with him, and deliver them the prisoners. 71Which thing he accepted, and did according to his demands, and sware unto him that he would never do him harm all the days of his life. 72And he restored unto him the prisoners that he had taken aforetime out of the land of Judah; and turning back he returned, and went his way into his own land, neither came he any more into their lands. 73Thus the sword ceased from Israel, and Jonathan dwelt at Michmash, and began to govern the people; and he removed the ungodly men out of Israel.

CHAPTER 10

And in the hundred and sixtieth year Alexander the son of Antiochus, surnamed Epiphanes, went up and took Ptolemais. And the people accepted him, and he reigned there. 2Now when king Demetrius heard thereof, he gathered together an exceeding great host, and went forth against him to meet him in battle. 3Moreover Demetrius sent letters unto Jonathan, with loving words so as to magnify him. 4‘For’, said he, ‘let us move aforehand to make peace with him, before he join with Alexander against us. 5For he will remember all the evils that we have done against him, and against his brethren, and the nation.’ 6Wherefore he gave him authority to gather together a host, and to prepare weapons, and to be his friend. He commanded also that the hostages that were in the citadel should be delivered to him. 7Then came Jonathan to Jerusalem, and read the letters in the audience of all the people; and of them that were in the citadel. 8And they were sore afraid when they heard that the king had given him authority to gather together a host. 9Whereupon they of the citadel returned their hostages unto Jonathan, and he delivered them unto their parents. 10And Jonathan settled himself in Jerusalem, and began to build and repair the city. 11And he commanded the workmen to build the walls also round about mount Zion with square stones for fortification; and they did so. 12Then the Gentiles that were in the fortresses which Bacchides had built fled away. 13And every man left his place, and went into his own country. 14Only at Beth-Zur certain of those that had forsaken the law and the commandments remained still; for it became a place of refuge.

15Now king Alexander heard what promises Demetrius had sent unto Jonathan, and they told him of the battles and noble acts which he and his brethren had done, and of the pains that they had endured. 16And he said, ‘Shall we find such another man? Now therefore we will make him our friend and confederate.’ 17Upon this he wrote a letter, and sent it unto him according to these words, saying, 18 19 ‘King Alexander to his brother Jonathan, greeting: We have heard of thee, that thou art a man of great power, and meet to be our friend. 20Wherefore now this day we ordain thee to be the high priest of thy nation, and to be called the king’s friend’ (and therewithal he sent him a purple robe and a crown of gold); ‘and require thee to take our part and keep friendship with us.’ 21So in the seventh month of the hundred and sixtieth year, at the feast of the tabernacles, Jonathan put on the holy robe, and gathered together forces, and provided many weapons.

22 23 Whereof when Demetrius heard, he was grieved and said, ‘What is this that we have done, that Alexander hath acted before us, in making amity with the Judeans to strengthen himself? 24I will also write unto them words of encouragement, and promise them dignities and gifts, that they may be my stay.’ 25He sent unto them therefore to this effect: ‘King Demetrius unto the people of the Judeans, greeting: 26Whereas ye have kept your covenants with us, and continued in our friendship, not joining yourselves with our enemies, we have heard thereof, and are glad. 27Wherefore now continue ye to keep faith with us, and we will well recompense you for the things ye do in our behalf, 28and will grant you many immunities, and give you rewards. 29And now do I release you, and for your sake I exempt all the Judeans from tributes, and from the customs of salt, and from crown taxes. 30And from that which appertaineth unto me to receive for the third part of the seed, and the half of the fruit of the trees, I release it from this day forth; so that they shall not be taken of the land of Judah, nor of the three governments which are added thereunto out of the country of Samaria and Galilee, from this day forth for evermore. 31Let Jerusalem also be holy and exempt, with the borders thereof, both from tithes and tributes. 32And as for the citadel, which is at Jerusalem, I yield up my authority over it, and give it to the high priest, that he may set in it such men as he shall choose to keep it. 33Moreover I freely set at liberty every one of the Judeans that were carried captive out of the land of Judah into any part of my kingdom, and I will that all my officers remit the tributes even of their cattle. 34Moreover, all the feasts, the Sabbaths, the new moons, the appointed days, as well as the three days before the feast and the three days after the feast, let all these days be established as of immunity and freedom for all the Judeans in my realm. 35Also no man shall have authority to meddle with them, or to molest any of them in any matter. 36Further, let there be enrolled among the king’s forces about thirty thousand men of the Judeans, unto whom pay shall be given as belongeth to all the king’s forces. 37And of them some shall be placed in the king’s strongholds, of whom also some shall be set over the affairs of the kingdom, which are of trust. And let their overseers and governors be of themselves, and that they live after their own laws, even as the king hath commanded in the land of Judah. 38And concerning the three governments that are added to Judah from the country of Samaria, let them be joined with Judah, that they may be reckoned to be under one, nor bound to obey other authority than the high priest’s. 39As for Ptolemais and the land pertaining thereto, I give it as a free gift to the sanctuary at Jerusalem, for the necessary expenses of the sanctuary. 40Moreover I give every year fifteen thousand shekels of silver out of the account from the places appertaining. 41And all the surplus, which the officers paid not in as in former time, from henceforth shall be given toward the works of the temple. 42And beside this, the five thousand shekels of silver which they took from the uses of the temple out of the accounts year by year, even those things shall be released, because they appertain to the priests that minister. 43And whosoever they be that flee unto the temple at Jerusalem, or be within the liberties thereof, being indebted unto the king or any other matter, let them be at liberty, and all that they have in my realm. 44For the building also, and repairing of the works of the sanctuary, expenses shall be given out of the king’s accounts, as also for the building of the walls in Judah. 45Yea, and for the building of the walls of Jerusalem, and the fortifying thereof round about, expenses shall be given out of the king’s accounts, as also for the building of the walls in Judah.’

46Now when Jonathan and the people heard these words, they gave no credit unto them, nor received them, because they had remembered the great evil that he had done in Israel, and how he had afflicted them very sore. 47But with Alexander they were well pleased, because he was the first that entreated of true peace with them; and they were confederate with him always.

48Then gathered king Alexander great forces, and camped over against Demetrius. 49And the two kings joined battle, and Demetrius’ host fled, and Alexander followed after him, and prevailed against them. 50And he continued the battle very sore until the sun went down, and that day Demetrius fell. 51And Alexander sent ambassadors to Ptolemy king of Egypt with a message to this effect: 52‘Forasmuch as I am come again to my realm, and am set in the throne of my progenitors, and have gotten the dominion, and crushed Demetrius, and recovered our country (53for I had joined battle with him, both he and his host was discomfited by us, so that we sit in the throne of his kingdom); 54now therefore let us make a league of amity together, and give me now thy daughter to wife, and I will be thy son-in-law and will give both thee and her gifts according to thy dignity’. 55Then Ptolemy the king gave answer, saying, ‘Happy be the day wherein thou didst return into the land of thy fathers, and satest on the throne of their kingdoms. 56And now will I do to thee as thou hast written. Meet me therefore at Ptolemais, that we may see one another; for I will marry my daughter to thee according to thy desire.’

57So Ptolemy went out of Egypt with his daughter Cleopatra, and they came unto Ptolemais in the hundred threescore and second year; 58where, king Alexander meeting him, he gave unto him his daughter Cleopatra, and celebrated her marriage at Ptolemais with great glory, as the manner of kings is. 59Then king Alexander wrote unto Jonathan to meet him; 60who thereupon went honourably to Ptolemais; and there he met the two kings, and gave them and their friends silver and gold and many presents, and found favour in their sight. 61At that time certain pestilent fellows of Israel, men of a wicked life, assembled themselves against him, to accuse him; but the king would not hear them. 62Yea more than that, the king commanded to take off his garments, and clothe him in purple; and they did so. 63Also he made him sit by himself, and said unto his princes, ‘Go with him into the midst of the city, and make proclamation that no man complain against him of any matter, and that no man trouble him for any manner of cause’. 64Now when his accusers saw that he was honoured according to the proclamation, and clothed in purple, they fled all away. 65So the king honoured him, and wrote him among his close friends, and made him a governor and partaker of his dominion. 66Then Jonathan returned to Jerusalem with peace and gladness.

67Furthermore in the hundred threescore and fifth year came Demetrius son of Demetrius out of Crete into the land of his fathers; 68whereof when king Alexander heard tell, he was right sorry, and returned into Antioch. 69Then Demetrius made Apollonius the governor of Celosyria; who gathered together a great host and camped in Jamnia, and sent unto Jonathan the high priest, saying, 70‘Thou alone liftest up thyself against us; and I was laughed to scorn for thy sake and reproached. And why dost thou have authority over us in the mountains? 71Now therefore, if thou trustest in thine own strength, come down to us into the plain field, and there let us ourselves try the matter together; for with me is the army of the cities. 72Ask, and learn who I am, and who the rest are that take our part, and they shall tell thee that thy foot is not able to stand before our face, for thy fathers have been twice put to flight in their own land. 73Wherefore now, thou shalt not be able to abide the horsemen and so great a power in the plain, where there is neither stone, nor flint, nor any place to flee unto.’

74So when Jonathan heard these words of Apollonius, he was moved in his mind, and choosing ten thousand men he went out of Jerusalem, where Simon his brother met him for to help him. 75And he pitched his tents against Joppa; but they shut him out of the city, because Apollonius had a garrison in Joppa. 76Then Jonathan made war against them, whereupon they of the city let him in for fear, and so Jonathan won Joppa. 77Whereof when Apollonius heard, he took three thousand horsemen with a great host, and went to Ashdod as one that journeyed; and therewithal he drew him forth into the plain, because he had a great number of horsemen, and put his trust therein. 78Then Jonathan followed after him to Ashdod, and the armies joined battle. 79Now Apollonius had left a thousand horsemen in ambush. 80And Jonathan knew that there was an ambushment behind him; for they had compassed in his host and cast darts at the people from morning till evening. 81But the people stood still, as Jonathan had commanded them, and the enemies’ horsemen grew tired. 82Then brought Simon forth his host, and set them against the phalanx (for the horsemen were spent), and they were crushed by him, and fled. 83The horsemen also were scattered in the field, and fled to Ashdod, and went into Beth-Dagon, their idol’s temple, for safety. 84But Jonathan set fire to Ashdod and the cities round about, and took their spoils; and the temple of Dagon with them that were in it he burned with fire. 85Thus were there well nigh eight thousand men that fell together with them that were burned. 86And from thence Jonathan departed, and camped against Askalon; and the men of the city came forth and met him with great pomp. 87And Jonathan returned unto Jerusalem with them that were with him having many spoils. 88Now it came to pass, when king Alexander heard these things, that he honoured Jonathan yet more, 89and sent him a buckle of gold, as the use is to be given to such as are of the king’s blood. He gave him also Ekron with the borders thereof in possession.

CHAPTER 11

And the king of Egypt gathered together a great host, like the sand that lieth upon the seashore, and many ships, and went about through deceit to get Alexander’s kingdom, and to join it to his own. 2Whereupon he took his journey into Syria with words of peace; and they of the cities opened to him and met him, for king Alexander had commanded them so to do, because he was his father-in-law. 3Now as Ptolemy entered into the cities, he set in every one of them a garrison of soldiers to keep it. 4And when he came near to Ashdod, they shewed him the temple of Dagon that was burnt, and Ashdod and the suburbs thereof that were destroyed, and the bodies that were cast abroad, and them that Jonathan had burnt in the battle; for they had made heaps of them by the way where he should pass. 5Also they told the king whatsoever Jonathan had done, to the intent he might blame him; but the king held his peace. 6Then Jonathan met the king with great pomp at Joppa, where they greeted one another, and lodged. 7And Jonathan journeyed with the king to the river called Eleutherus, and returned again to Jerusalem. 8King Ptolemy therefore, having gotten the dominion of the cities by the sea unto Seleucia upon the sea coast, imagined wicked counsels against Alexander. 9And he sent ambassadors unto king Demetrius, saying, ‘Come, let us make a league betwixt us, and I will give thee my daughter, whom Alexander hath, and thou shalt reign over thy father’s kingdom; 10for I repent that I gave my daughter unto him, for he sought to slay me’. 11Thus did he blame him, because he was desirous of his kingdom. 12Wherefore he took back his daughter, and gave her to Demetrius; wherefore he was estranged from Alexander, so that their hatred was openly known. 13Then Ptolemy entered into Antioch, and put on the crown of Asia, and he set two crowns upon his head, that of Egypt and of Asia. 14But king Alexander was in Cilicia, because those that dwelt in those parts had revolted. 15But Alexander heard of this, and he came against him to war; and king Ptolemy brought forth his host, and met him with a mighty power, and put him to flight. 16And Alexander fled into Arabia, there to hide himself, and king Ptolemy was exalted. 17And Zabdiel the Arabian took off Alexander’s head, and sent it to Ptolemy. 18King Ptolemy also died the third day after, and they that were in the strongholds were destroyed by them that dwelt in those strongholds. 19And Demetrius became king in the hundred threescore and seventh year.

20In those days, Jonathan gathered together men from Judah to assail the citadel that was in Jerusalem, and he made many engines of war against it. 21Then certain ungodly persons, who hated their own people, went unto the king, and told him that Jonathan had besieged the citadel. 22Whereof when he heard, he was angry, and immediately removing he came to Ptolemais, and wrote unto Jonathan that he should not lay siege to the tower, but come and speak with him at Ptolemais in great haste. 23Nevertheless Jonathan, when he heard this, commanded to besiege it still. And he chose certain of the elders of Israel and the priests, and put himself in peril, 24and took silver and gold, and raiment, and divers presents besides, and went to Ptolemais unto the king, and found favour in his sight. 25And yet again certain ungodly men of the nation had made complaint against him; 26yet the king entreated him as his predecessors had done before, and promoted him in the sight of all his friends. 27He also confirmed him in the high priesthood and whatsoever honours that he had before, and made him to be accounted among his chief friends. 28Then Jonathan desired the king that he would make Judah free from tribute, as also the three governments with the country of Samaria, and he promised him three hundred talents. 29And the king was pleased, and wrote letters unto Jonathan of all these things after this manner: 30‘King Demetrius unto his brother Jonathan, and unto the nation of the Judeans, greeting: 31We send you here this copy of the letter which we did write unto our cousin Lasthenes concerning you, that ye might know what is therein. 32“King Demetrius unto his father Lasthenes, greeting: 33We are determined to do good to the people of the Judeans, who are our friends and keep covenants with us, because of their goodwill toward us. 34Wherefore we have ratified unto them the borders of Judah, with the three governments of Aphairema and Lydda and Rathamin that are added unto Judah from the country of Samaria, and all things appertaining unto them, for all such as do sacrifice in Jerusalem, instead of the payments which the king received of them yearly aforetime out of the fruits of the earth and of trees. 35And as for other things that belong unto us of the tithes and customs pertaining unto us, as also the saltpits, and the crown taxes which are due unto us, we discharge of them all for their relief. 36And nothing hereof shall be revoked from this time forth for ever. 37Now therefore see that thou make a copy of these things, and let it be delivered unto Jonathan, and set upon the holy mount in a conspicuous place.” ’

38After this, when king Demetrius saw that the land was quiet before him, and that no man opposed him, he sent away all his forces every one to his own place except certain bands of Gentiles, whom he had gathered from the isles of the nations; but all the forces that had served his fathers hated him. 39Moreover one Trypho was one them that had been of Alexander’s part afore; who, seeing that all the host murmured against Demetrius, went to Imalkue the Arabian that brought up Antiochus the young son of Alexander. 40And he lay sore upon him to deliver him this young Antiochus, that he might reign in his father’s stead. He told him therefore all that Demetrius had done, and how his men of war were at enmity with him, and there he remained a long season. 41And Jonathan sent unto king Demetrius, requesting that he would cast those of the tower out of Jerusalem, and those also in the fortresses; for they fought against Israel. 42So Demetrius sent unto Jonathan, saying, ‘I will not only do this for thee and thy people, but I will greatly honour thee and thy nation if opportunity serve. 43Now therefore thou shalt do well, if thou send me men who will be confederate with me; for all my forces have revolted from me.’ 44Jonathan therefore sent him three thousand men powerful in strength unto Antioch; and when they came to the king, the king was very glad of their coming. 45Howbeit they that were of the city gathered themselves together into the midst of the city, to the number of an hundred and twenty thousand men, and would have slain the king. 46Wherefore the king fled into the court; but they of the city kept the passages of the city, and began to fight. 47Then the king called to the Judeans for help, who came unto him all at once, and dispersing themselves through the city, slew that day to the number of an hundred thousand. 48Also they set fire to the city, and got many spoils that day, and delivered the king. 49So they of the city saw that the Judeans had got the city as they would, and their courage was abated, and they cried unto the king and made supplication, saying, 50‘Grant us peace, and let the Judeans cease from assaulting us and the city.” 51With that they cast away their weapons, and made peace; and the Judeans were honoured in the sight of the king, and in the sight of all that were in his realm; and they returned to Jerusalem having great spoils. 52So king Demetrius sat on the throne of his kingdom, and the land was quiet before him. 53Nevertheless he dissembled in all that ever he spake, and estranged himself from Jonathan; neither rewarded he him according to the benefits which he had received of him, but troubled him very sore.

54After this returned Trypho, and with him the young child Antiochus; and he reigned and put on the crown. 55Then there gathered unto him all the men of war whom Demetrius had put away; and they fought against Demetrius, who turned his back and fled. 56Moreover Trypho took the elephants, and won Antioch. 57So young Antiochus wrote unto Jonathan, saying, ‘I confirm thee in the high priesthood, and appoint thee ruler over the four governments, and to be one of the king’s friends’. 58He sent him also golden vessels to serve him at table, and gave him authority to drink from these, to be clothed in purple and to wear a golden buckle. 59His brother Simon also he made captain, from the place called The Ladder of Tyre unto the borders of Egypt. 60Then Jonathan went forth, and journeyed beyond the river and in the cities; and all the forces of Syria gathered themselves unto him for to help him. And when he came to Askalon, they of the city met him honourably. 61From thence he went to Gaza, but they of Gaza shut him out; wherefore he laid siege unto it, and burned the suburbs thereof with fire, and spoiled them. 62Therefore they of Gaza made supplication unto Jonathan; and he made peace with them, and took the sons of their chief men for hostages and passed through the country unto Damascus. 63Now Jonathan heard that Demetrius’ princes were come to Kadesh in Galilee with a great power, purposing to remove him out of the country. 64And he went to meet them, and left Simon his brother in the country. 65Then Simon encamped against Beth-Zur, and fought against it a long season and shut it up. 66But they desired to have peace with him; and he granted it them. And then he put them out from thence, and took the city, and put a garrison in it. 67And Jonathan and his host pitched at the water of Gennesaret, and early in the morning they gat them to the plain of Hazor. 68And behold, the host of Gentiles met them in the plain, and they laid men in ambush for him in the mountains, but came themselves over against him. 69So they that lay in ambush rose out of their places and joined battle, and all that were of Jonathan’s side fled; 70there was not one of them left except Mattathias the son of Absalom and Judah the son of Chalphi, the captains of the host. 71Then Jonathan rent his clothes, and cast earth upon his head, and prayed. 72Then turning again to battle, he put them to flight, and so they ran away. 73Now when his own men that were fled saw this, they turned again unto him, and with him pursued them to Kadesh, even unto their own tents, and there they camped. 74So there were slain of the nations that day about three thousand men; and Jonathan returned to Jerusalem.

CHAPTER 12

And Jonathan saw that the time served him; and he chose certain men, and sent them to Rome, for to confirm and renew the friendship that they had with them. 2He sent letters also to the Lacedemonians and to other places, for the same purpose. 3So they went unto Rome, and entered into the senate, and said, ‘Jonathan the high priest, and the people of the Judeans, sent us here to renew the friendship which ye had with them and league as in former time’. 4Then the Romans gave them letters unto the governors of every place that they should bring them into the land of Judah peaceably.

5And this is the copy of the letters which Jonathan wrote to the Lacedemonians: 6‘Jonathan the high priest, and the elders of the nation, and the priests, and the other people of the Judeans, unto the Lacedemonians their brethren, greeting: 7There were letters sent in times past unto Onias, the high priest from Darius, who reigned then among you, to signify that ye are our brethren, as the copy here underwritten doth specify. 8And Onias entreated the ambassador that was sent honourably, and received the letters wherein declaration was made of the league and friendship. 9Therefore we also, albeit we need none of these things, for that we take comfort in the holy books of scripture in our hands to comfort us, 10have attempted to send unto you for the renewing of brotherhood and friendship, lest we should become strangers unto you altogether; for there is a long time passed since ye sent word unto us. 11We therefore at all times without ceasing, both in our feasts, and other convenient days, do remember you in the sacrifices which we offer, and in our prayers, as reason is, and as it becometh us to think upon our brethren; 12 13 and we are right glad of your glory. But great troubles have compassed us about on every side, and the kings that are round about us have fought against us. 14Howbeit we would not be troublesome unto you, nor to others of our confederates and friends, in these wars; 15for we have help from heaven that succoureth us, so as we were delivered from our enemies; and our enemies were humbled. 16For this cause we chose Numenius the son of Antiochus and Antipater the son of Jason, and sent them unto the Romans, to renew the amity that we had with them, and the former league. 17We commanded them also to go unto you, and to greet you, and to deliver you our letters concerning the renewing of our brotherhood. 18Wherefore now ye shall do well to give us an answer thereto.’

19 20 And this is the copy of the letters which they sent to Onias:‘Areus king of the Lacedemonians to Onias the high priest, greeting: 21It is found in writing that the Lacedemonians and Judeans are brethren, and that they are of the stock of Abraham. 22Now therefore, since this is come to our knowledge, ye shall do well to write unto us of your prosperity. 23Therefore we do write back again to you, that your cattle and goods are ours, and ours are yours. 24Then Jonathan heard that Demetrius’ princes were come to fight against him with a greater host than afore. And he removed from Jerusalem, and met them in the land of Hamath; for he gave them no respite to enter his country. 25He sent spies also unto their tents, who came again and told him that they were appointed to come upon him in the night season. 27Wherefore so soon as the sun was down Jonathan commanded his men to watch, and to be in arms, that all the night long they might be ready to fight; also he sent sentinels round about the host. 28But the adversaries learned that Jonathan and his men were ready for battle, and they feared and trembled in their hearts, and they kindled fires in their camps. 29Howbeit Jonathan and his company knew it not till the morning, for they saw the lights burning. 30Then Jonathan pursued after them, but overtook them not; for they were gone over the river Eleutherus. 31Wherefore Jonathan turned to the Arabians, who were called Zabadeans, and smote them, and took their spoils. 32And removing thence, he came to Damascus, and so passed through all that country. 33Simon also went forth, and passed through the country unto Askalon and the strongholds there adjoining; and he turned aside to Joppa, and won it. 34For he had heard that they would deliver the stronghold unto them that took Demetrius’ part; wherefore he set a garrison there to keep it. 35After this came Jonathan home again, and calling the elders of the people together, he consulted with them about building strongholds in Judah, 36and making the walls of Jerusalem higher, and raising a great mount between the tower and the city, for to separate it from the city, that so it might be alone, that the garrison might neither sell nor buy in it. 37So they came together to build up the city, forasmuch as part of the wall toward the brook on the east side was fallen down; and they repaired that which was called Chaphenatha. 38Simon also set up Adida in Shephelah, and made it strong with gates and bars.

39Now Trypho went about to rule Asia, to set the crown upon his own head, and to kill Antiochus the king. 40Howbeit he was afraid that Jonathan would not suffer him, and that he would fight against him; wherefore he sought a way how to take Jonathan, that he might kill him. So he removed and came to Beth-Shan. 41Then Jonathan went out to meet him with forty thousand men chosen for the battle and came to Beth-Shan. 42And Trypho saw that Jonathan came with so great a force; and he durst not stretch out his hand against him, 43but received him honourably, and commended him unto all his friends, and gave him gifts, and commanded his men of war to be as obedient unto him as to himself. 44Unto Jonathan also he said, ‘Why hast thou put all this people to so great trouble, seeing there is no war betwixt us? 45Therefore send them now home again, and choose a few men to wait on thee, and come thou with me to Ptolemais; and I will give it thee, and the rest of the strongholds and forces, and all that have any charge. As for me, I will return and depart, for this is the cause of my coming.’ 46So Jonathan, believing him, did as he bade him; he sent away his host, and they went into the land of Judah. 47And with himself he retained but three thousand men, of whom he sent two thousand into Galilee, and one thousand went with him. 48Now as soon as Jonathan entered into Ptolemais, they of Ptolemais shut the gates, and took him; and all them that came with him they slew with the sword. 49Then sent Trypho a host of footmen and horsemen into Galilee, and into the great plain, to destroy all Jonathan’s company. 50But when they knew that Jonathan and they that were with him were taken and slain, they encouraged one another, and went close together prepared to fight. 51They therefore that followed upon them, perceiving that they were ready to fight for their lives, turned back again. 52Wherefore they all came into the land of Judah peaceably, and there they bewailed Jonathan, and them that were with him; and they were sore afraid, wherefore all Israel made great lamentation. 53Then all the nations that were round about them sought to destroy them; for said they, ‘They have no captain nor any to help them; now therefore let us make war upon them and take away their memorial from among men’.

CHAPTER 13

Now Simon heard that Trypho had gathered together a great host to invade the land of Judah and devastate it; 2and he saw that the people was in great trembling and fear; and he went up into Jerusalem, and gathered the people together, 3and gave them exhortation, saying, ‘Ye yourselves know all that I and my brethren and my father’s house have done for the law and the sanctuary, the battles also and troubles which we have seen, 4by reason whereof all my brethren are slain for Israel’s sake, and I am left alone. 5Now therefore, be it far from me that I should spare my life in any time of trouble, for I am no better than my brethren. 6Doubtless I will avenge my nation, and the sanctuary, and our wives and our children; for the nations are gathered to destroy us out of very malice.” 7And the spirit the people revived when they heard these words. 8And they answered with a loud voice, saying, ‘Thou shalt be our leader, instead of Judah and Jonathan thy brother. 9Fight thou our battles, and whatsoever thou commandest us that will we do.’ 10So then he gathered together all the men of war, and made haste to finish the walls of Jerusalem, and he fortified it round about. 11Also he sent Jonathan the son of Absalom, and with him a great power to Joppa; who, casting out them that were therein, remained there in it.

12So Trypho removed from Ptolemais with a great power, to enter into the land of Judah; and Jonathan was with him under guard. 13But Simon pitched his tents at Adida over against the plain. 14Now Trypho knew that Simon was risen up instead of his brother Jonathan, and that he meant to join battle with him; and he sent messengers unto him, saying, 15‘We have Jonathan thy brother in prison by reason of the money that he is owing unto the king’s treasure, concerning the business that was committed unto him. 16Wherefore now send an hundred talents of silver, and two of his sons for hostage, that when he is at liberty he may not revolt from us, and we will let him go.’ 17Hereupon Simon, albeit he perceived that they spake deceitfully unto him, yet sent he the money and the sons, lest peradventure he should procure to himself great hatred of the people, 18who might have said, ‘Because I sent him not the money and the sons, therefore is 19 Jonathan dead’.So he sent them the sons, and the hundred talents; howbeit Trypho dissembled, neither would he let Jonathan go. 20And after this came Trypho to invade the land and destroy it, going round about by the way that leadeth unto Adora; but Simon and his host marched over against him in every place wheresoever he went. 21Now they that were in the tower sent messengers unto Trypho, to the end that he should hasten his coming unto them by the wilderness, and send them victuals. 22Wherefore Trypho made ready all his horsemen to come that night; but there fell a very great snow, by reason whereof he came not. So he departed, and came into the country of Gilead. 23And when he came near to Bascama, he slew Jonathan; and he was buried there. 24And Trypho returned, and went into his own land.

25Then sent Simon, and took the bones of Jonathan his brother, and buried him in Modein, the city of his fathers. 26And all Israel made great lamentation for him, and bewailed him many days. 27Simon also built a monument upon the sepulchre of his father and his brethren, and raised it aloft to the sight, with hewn stone behind and before. 28Moreover he set up seven pyramids, one against another, for his father and his mother and his four brethren. 29And in these he made cunning devices, about which he set great pillars; and upon the pillars he made all their armour for a perpetual memory, and by the armour ships carved, that they might be seen of all that sail on the sea. 30This sepulchre, which he made at Modein, standeth yet unto this day.

31Now Trypho dealt deceitfully with the young king Antiochus, and slew him. 32And he reigned in his stead, and put on the crown of of Asia, and brought a great calamity upon the land. 33Then Simon built up the strongholds in Judah, and fenced them about with high towers, and great walls, and gates and bars, and laid up victuals therein. 34Moreover Simon chose men, and sent to king Demetrius, to the end that he should give the land relief, because all that Trypho did was to spoil. 35 36 And king Demetrius answered and wrote after this manner: ‘King Demetrius unto Simon, the high priest and friend of kings, as also unto the elders and nation of the Judeans, greeting: 37The golden crown, and the branch of the palm tree, which ye sent unto us, we have received; and we are ready to make a steadfast peace with you, yea, and to write unto our officers to exempt you from taxes. 38And whatsoever covenants we have made with you shall stand, and the strongholds which ye have built shall be your own. 39As for any oversight or fault committed unto this day, we forgive it and the crown tax also which ye owe us; and if there were any other tribute paid in Jerusalem, it shall no more be paid. 40And if there be some which are deserving among you to be enrolled among our court, let them be enrolled, and let there be peace betwixt us.’ 41And the yoke of the nations was taken away from Israel in the hundred and seventieth year. 42Then the people of Israel began to write in their instruments and contracts: ‘In the first year of Simon the high priest, the governor and leader of the Judeans’. 43In those days Simon camped against Gaza, and compassed it with soldiers round about; he made also an engine of war and set it by the city and battered a certain tower and took it. 44And they that were in the engine leaped into the city; and there was a great uproar in the city. 45And the people of the city rent their clothes, and climbed upon the walls with their wives and children, and cried with a loud voice, beseeching Simon to grant them peace. 46And they said, ‘Deal not with us according to our wickedness, but according to thy mercy’. 47So Simon was appeased toward them, and fought no more against them, but put them out of the city, and cleansed the houses wherein the idols were, and so entered into it with songs and thanksgiving. 48Yea, he put all uncleanness out of it, and placed such men there as would keep the law, and made it stronger than it was before, and built therein a dwelling-place for himself. 49They also that were in the citadel in Jerusalem were prevented, so that they could neither come forth nor go into the country to buy and sell. Wherefore they greatly hungered, and a great number of them perished through famine. 50Then cried they to Simon, beseeching him to be at one with them; and so he granted them. And he put them out from thence, and cleansed the citadel from pollutions. 51And they entered into it the three and twentieth day of the second month, in the hundred seventy and first year, with thanksgiving, and branches of palm trees; and with harps, and with lyres and cymbals, and string instruments and songs, because there was destroyed a great enemy out of Israel. 52He ordained also that that day should be kept every year with gladness. Moreover the mount of the temple that was by the citadel, he made stronger than it was; and there he dwelt himself with his company. 53And when Simon saw that John his son was a man, he made him captain of all the hosts, and he dwelt in Gazara.

CHAPTER 14

Now in the hundred threescore and twelfth year king Demetrius gathered his forces together and went into Media to get him help to fight against Trypho. 2But when Arsaces, the king of Persia and Media, heard that Demetrius was entered within his borders, he sent one of his princes to take him alive; 3who went, and smote the host of Demetrius, and took him and brought him to Arsaces; by whom he was put in prison.

4The land of Judah was quiet all the days of Simon, and he sought the good of his nation in such wise, and his authority and his glory pleased them well. 5And Then in all his glory he took Joppa for a haven, and made an entrance to the isles of the sea; 6he enlarged the bounds of his nation, and recovered the country, 7and gathered together a great number of captives, and had the dominion of Gazara and Beth-Zur and the citadel. And out of it he took all uncleanness, and there was not any that resisted him. 8Then did they till their ground in peace; and the earth gave her increase, and the trees of the field their fruit. 9The ancient men sat in the streets, communing together of good things; and the young men put on the glories and apparel of war. 10He provided victuals for the cities, and set in them all manner of munition, until his glorious name was renowned unto the ends of the earth. 11He made peace in the land, and Israel rejoiced with great joy. 12Every man sat under his vine and his fig tree, and there was none to frighten them. 13He made war to cease in the land to fight; yea, the kings themselves were overthrown in those days. 14Moreover he strengthened all those of his people that were brought low; the law he searched out, and every transgressor of the law and wicked person he took away. 15He beautified the sanctuary, and multiplied the vessels of the sanctuary.

16Now when it was heard at Rome, and as far as Sparta, that Jonathan was dead, they were very sorry. 17But as soon as they heard that his brother Simon was made high priest in his stead, and ruled the country and the cities therein, 18they wrote unto him in tablets of brass, to renew the friendship and league which they had made with Judah and Jonathan his brethren; 19which writings were read before the congregation at Jerusalem. 20And this is the copy of the letters that the Lacedomonians sent: ‘The rulers of the Lacedomonians with the city unto Simon the high priest, and the elders and priests and residue of the people of the Judeans, our brethren, greeting. 21The ambassadors that were sent unto our people certified us of your glory and honour; wherefore we were glad of their coming, 22and did register the things that they spake in the council of the people in this manner: “Numenius son of Antiochus, and Antipater son of Jason, the Judeans’ ambassadors, came unto us, to renew the friendship they had with us. 23And it pleased the people to entertain the men honourably, and to put the copy of their embassy in public records, to the end the people of the Lacedomonians might have a memorial thereof. Furthermore they have written a copy thereof unto Simon the high priest.” ’ 24After this Simon sent Numenius to Rome, with a great shield of gold of a thousand pounds weight, to confirm the league with them.

25Whereof when the people heard, they said, ‘What thanks shall we give to Simon and his son? 26For he and his two brethren and the house of his father stood sure, and fought the enemies of Israel, and confirmed her liberty.” So then they wrote it in tablets of brass, which they set upon pillars in mount Zion; 27and this is the copy of the writing: ‘The eighteenth day of the month Elul, in the hundred threescore and twelfth year, being the third year of Simon the high priest, 28at Saramel in the great congregation of the priests and people, and rulers of the nation, and elders of the country, were these things notified unto us. 29Forasmuch as oftentimes there have been wars in the country, wherein for the maintenance of their sanctuary and the law, Simon the son of Mattathias, of the posterity of Jarib, together with his brethren, put themselves in jeopardy, and resisted the adversaries of their nation, that the sanctuary and the law might endure; and they glorified their nation with great glory. 30And Jonathan gathered his nation together, and became their high priest, and was added to his people. 31And their enemies purposed to invade their country, that they might destroy it and lay hands on the sanctuary. 32At which time Simon resisted, and fought for his nation, and spent much of his own substance, and armed the valiant men of his nation, and gave them wages. 33And he fortified the cities of Judah, together with Beth-Zur that lieth upon the borders of Judah, where the armour of the enemies had been before; but he set a garrison of Judeans there. 34Moreover he fortified Joppa, which lieth upon the sea, and Gazara, that bordereth upon Ashdod, where the enemies had dwelt before; but he placed the Judeans there, and furnished them with all things convenient for the reparation thereof. 35The people therefore, seeing the acts of Simon, and unto what glory he sought to bring his nation, made him their governor and chief priest, because he had done all these things, and for the justice and faith which he kept to his nation, and for that he sought by all means to honour his nation. 36For in his time things prospered in his hands, and he drave the nations out of their country. And they also that were in the city of Jerusalem had made themselves a citadel, out of which they issued and polluted all about the sanctuary, and did much hurt in the holy place. 37And he placed Judeans therein, and fortified it for the safety of the country and the city, and raised up the walls of Jerusalem. 38King Demetrius also confirmed him in the high priesthood, according to those things, 39and made him one of his friends, and honoured him with great honour. 40For he had heard say that the Romans had called the Judeans their friends and confederates and brethren, and that they had entertained the ambassadors of Simon with honour; 41also that the Judeans and priests were well-pleased that Simon should be their governor and high priest for ever, until there should arise a faithful prophet; 42moreover that he should be their captain, and should take charge of the sanctuary, to set them over their works, and over the country, and over the armour, and over the fortresses; and that he should take charge of the sanctuary; 43and that he should be obeyed of every man, and that all his writings in the country should be made in his name, and that he should be clothed in purple and gold. 44Also that none of the people or priests should break any of these things, or set aside his word, or gather an assembly in the country without him, or to be clothed in purple, or wear a buckle of gold. 45Whosoever should do otherwise or break any of these things, he should be held liable.’ 46Thus it pleased all the people to deal with Simon, and to do according to all these words. 47Then Simon accepted hereof, and was well pleased to be high priest and captain and governor of the Judeans, and to defend them all. 48So they commanded that this writing should be put in tablets of brass, and that they should be set up within the compass of the sanctuary in a conspicuous place; 49also that the copies thereof should be laid up in the treasury, to the end that Simon and his sons might have them.

CHAPTER 15

Moreover Antiochus son of Demetrius the king sent letters from the isles of the sea unto Simon, the priest and prince of the Judeans, and to all the people; 2the contents whereof were these: ‘King Antiochus to Simon the high priest and prince of his nation, and to the people of the Judeans, greeting. 3Forasmuch as certain pestilent men have usurped the kingdom of our fathers, and my purpose is to challenge it again, that I may restore it to the old estate, and to that end have gathered a multitude of foreign soldiers together, and prepared ships of war; 4my meaning also being to go through the country, that I may be avenged of them that have destroyed it, and made many cities in the kingdom desolate: 5now therefore I confirm unto thee all the oblations, which the kings before me granted thee, and whatsoever gifts beside they granted. 6I give thee leave also to coin money for thy country with thine own stamp, 7and for Jerusalem and the sanctuary to be free. And all the armour that thou hast made, and fortresses that thou hast built and keepest in thine hands, let them remain unto thee. 8And any thing be or shall be owing to the king, let them be remitted for evermore. 9Furthermore, when we have obtained our kingdom, we will honour thee and thy nation and thy temple with great glory, so that your glory shall be evident throughout the world.’ 10In the hundred threescore and fourteenth year went Antiochus into the land of his fathers, at which time all the forces came together unto him, so that few were left with Trypho. 11Wherefore when king Antiochus pursued after him, he fled unto Dora, which lieth by the sea side; 12for he saw that evils came upon him all at once, and his forces forsook him. 13Then camped Antiochus against Dora, having with him an hundred and twenty thousand men of war, and eight thousand horsemen. 14And he compassed the city round about; and the ships joined battle from the sea side; and he vexed the city by land and by sea, neither suffered he any to go out or in.

15In the mean season came Numenius, and them that were with him from Rome, having letters to the kings and countries, wherein were written these things: 16 17 ‘Lucius, consul of the Romans, unto king Ptolemy, greeting. The Judeans’ ambassador, our friends and confederates, came unto us to renew the old friendship and league, being sent from Simon the high priest, and from the people of the Judeans, 18and they brought a shield of gold of a thousand pound. 19We thought it good therefore to write unto the kings and countries, that they should do them no harm nor fight against them, their cities or countries, nor yet aid their enemies against them. 20It seemed also good to us to receive the shield of them. 21If therefore there be any pestilent fellows that have fled from their country unto you, deliver them unto Simon the high priest, that he may punish them according to their own law.’ 22The same things wrote he likewise unto Demetrius the king and Attalus, to Ariarathes and Arsaces, 23and to all the countries, and to Sampsames and the Lacedemonians, and to Delus and Myndus, and Sieyon and Caria, and Samos and Pamphylia, and Lycia and Halicarnassus, and Rhodes and Phaselis, and Kos and Side, and Arados and Gortyna, and Cnidus and Cyprus and Cyrene. 24And the copy hereof they wrote to Simon the high priest.

25Now Antiochus the king camped against Dora a second time, assaulting it continually, and making engines; and he shut up Trypho, that he could neither go out nor in. 26At that time Simon sent Antiochus two thousand chosen men to aid him, silver also and gold and much armour. 27Nevertheless he would not receive them, but brake all the covenants which he had made with him afore, and became strange unto him. 28Furthermore he sent unto him Athenombaius, one of his friends, to commune with him and say, ‘Ye withhold Joppa and Gazara, with the citadel that is in Jerusalem, which are cities of my realm. 29The borders thereof ye have wasted, and done great hurt in the land, and got the dominion of many places within my kingdom. 30Now therefore deliver the cities which ye have taken, and the tributes of the places whereof ye have gotten dominion without the borders of Judah; 31or else give me for them five hundred talents of silver, and for the harm that ye have done and the tributes of the cities, another five hundred talents. If not, we will come and fight against you.’ 32So Athenombaius, the king’s friend, came to Jerusalem, and he saw the glory of Simon, and the cupboard of gold and silver plate, and his great attendance; and he was astonished, and told him the king’s message. 33Then answered Simon, and said unto him, ‘We have neither taken other men’s land, nor holden that which appertaineth to others, but the inheritance of our fathers, which our enemies have wrongfully in possession a certain time. 34 35 Wherefore we, having opportunity, hold the inheritance of our fathers. And whereas thou demandest Joppa and Gazara, albeit they did great harm unto the people in our country, yet will we give an hundred talents for them.’ Hereunto Athenombaius answered him not a word, 36but returned in a rage to the king, and made report unto him of these speeches, and of the glory of Simon, and of all that he had seen; whereupon the king was exceeding wroth.

37 38 In the mean time fled Trypho by ship unto Orthosias. Then the king made Cendebius captain of the sea coast, and gave him a host of footmen and horsemen, 39and commanded him to move his host toward Judah. Also he commanded him to build up Kedron, and to fortify the gates, and to war against the people; but as for the king himself, he pursued Trypho. 40So Cendebeus came to Jamnia, and began to provoke the people and to invade Judah, and to take the people prisoner, and to slay them. 41And he built up Kedron, and set horsemen there and footmen, to the end that they might go out and march along the ways of Judah, as the king had commanded him.

CHAPTER 16

Then came up John from Gazara, and told Simon his father what Cendebeus had done. 2Wherefore Simon called his two eldest sons, Judah and John, and said unto them, ‘I and my brethren and my father’s house have ever from our youth unto this day fought against the enemies of Israel. And we were successful, and delivered Israel by our hands oftentimes. 3But now I am old, and ye, by God’s mercy, are of a sufficient age; be ye instead of me and my brothers, and go and fight for our nation, and the help from heaven be with you.’ 4So he chose out of the country twenty thousand men of war with horsemen; and they went out against Cendebeus, and slept that night in Modein. 5And they rose in the morning, and went into the plain; and behold, a mighty great host both of footmen and horsemen came against them; howbeit there was a water brook betwixt them. 6So he and his people pitched over against them; and when he saw that the people were afraid to go over the water brook, he went first over himself; and then the men seeing him passed through after him. 7That done, he divided his men, and set the horsemen in the midst of the footmen; for the enemies’ horsemen were very many. 8Then sounded they with the holy trumpets, whereupon Cendebeus and his host were put to flight, so that many of them were slain, and the remnant gat them to the stronghold. 9At that time was Judah, John’s brother, wounded; but John still followed after them, until he came to Kedron, which Cendebeus had built. 10So they fled even unto the towers in the fields of Ashdod; wherefore he burned it with fire, so that there were slain of them about two thousand men.

11Moreover Ptolemeus the son of Abubus was made captain in the plain of Jericho; and he had abundance of silver and gold, 12for he was the high priest’s son- in-law. 13And his heart being lifted up, he thought to get the country to himself, and thereupon consulted deceitfully against Simon and his sons, to destroy them. 14Now Simon, when he was visiting the cities that were in the country, and taking care for the good ordering of them, he also came down himself to Jericho with his sons, Mattathias and Judah, in the hundred threescore and seventeenth year, in the eleventh month, called Shebat. 15And the son of Abubas received them deceitfully into his little stronghold called Dok, which he had built, and made them a great banquet; howbeit he had hid men there. 16And when Simon and his sons had drunk largely, Ptolemy and his men rose up, and took their weapons, and came upon Simon into the banqueting place, and slew him and his two sons and certain of his servants. 17In doing the which he committed a great treachery, and recompensed evil for good. 18Then Ptolemy wrote these things, and sent to the king, that he should send him a host to aid him, and he would deliver him the country and cities. 19He sent others also to Gazara to kill John, and unto the tribunes he sent letters to come unto him, that he might give them silver and gold and rewards. 20 21 And others he sent to take Jerusalem, and the mount of the temple. Now one had run afore to Gazara, and told John that his father and brethren were slain, and, quoth he, ‘He hath sent to slay thee also.’ 22Hereof when he heard, he was sore astonished; so he laid hands on them that were come to destroy him, and slew them; for he knew that they sought to make him away.

23As concerning the rest of the acts of John, and his wars and worthy deeds which he did, and the building of the walls which he made, and his doings, 24behold, these are written in the chronicles of the priesthood from the time he became high priest after his father.
